  <div class="prose col-span-full lg:col-span-8 xl:col-span-6">

  This has been our hardest year yet, and we won’t pretend otherwise.


  We celebrated our 10th anniversary in July, a milestone that should have felt purely celebratory. But just two months later, we made the difficult decision to make our two employee team members redundant. While we hope we handled it with care and dignity, it remains the toughest thing we’ve had to do in Kind’s history.


  We posted our biggest loss to date. In that context, continuing our 1% for the Planet commitment, donating cash and in-kind support despite posting a loss, might seem illogical. But it’s exactly these moments that test what we actually stand for versus what we claim to.


  We’ve just recertified as a B Corp, improving our scores in Governance, Community, Environment and Customers. Losing all our Workers points due to no longer having employees stung, not because of the score, but because it highlights how the framework struggles to recognise how we treat the other creatives and professionals we work with every day.


  Looking ahead, we’re hoping for some stability that will help us continue making a difference for the causes we care about. We want to get back to focusing on the work, deepening relationships with clients doing vital work on climate and social justice, and finding new ways to amplify their impact. The past year has tested us, but it hasn’t changed what we believe in or why we do this.


  The work itself, helping organisations like Artswork, Bookmark Reading, Rewilding Britain and The Wilderness Project create real change, remains the point. We’re still here. Still committed. Still learning what it actually costs to stay true to your values.
